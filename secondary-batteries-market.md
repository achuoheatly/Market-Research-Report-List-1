<p><p>The secondary batteries market comprises several key types, each with distinct characteristics. Lead-acid batteries are widely used for automotive and backup power due to their low cost and reliability. Lithium-ion batteries offer high energy density and longer life cycles, making them popular in consumer electronics and electric vehicles. Nickel-cadmium (Ni-Cd) batteries are known for their robustness but face environmental concerns. Nickel-metal hydride (Ni-MH) batteries provide better capacity than Ni-Cd and are commonly found in hybrid vehicles. Other emerging technologies include solid-state and flow batteries.</p></p>
